About West Africa

West Africa also called Western Africa and the West of Africa, is the westernmost region of Africa. With an estimated GDP value of US$1.483 trillion as at 2015, and also an estimated population of about 362 million people as of 2016, it has been defined as including 17 countries:

  • Benin
  • Burkina Faso
  • The Island nation of Cape Verde
  • Gambia
  • Ghana
  • Guinea
  • Guinea-Bissau
  • Ivory-Coast
  • Liberia
  • Mali
  • Mauritania
  • Niger
  • Nigeria
  • The Islands of Saint Helena
  • Ascension and Tristan da Cunha
  • Senegal
  • Sierra Leone
  • Togo.
